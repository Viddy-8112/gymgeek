## Protein: The Bare Essential

There's a common problem among gymgoers: They simply are not chowing enough protein. If you've ever questioned your own gym progress or wonder why you're not feeling optimal, you need to take a look at how much protein you're eating.<br> 

Protein is the fundamental building block of life. Every organic creature contains protein which is why it should be the first thing to consider in your everyday nutrition.<br>

--- 

## Reasons Why Protein is Important

1. ***Growth & Repair***<br> 
Your body uses protein to build and repair tissues in not only your muscles, but also your bones, skin and hair.<br>
If you haven't looked at the science of muscle building, essentially what you're doing in the gym is causing "micro-tears" in your muscles. Recovery means patching up these tears and rebuilding the muscles, making them grow stronger and bigger.<br> 
But where do you get the materials to rebuild? - From chowing protein bro!<br>

2. ***Satiety and Weight Management***<br>
Ever eat a meat and an hour later you get hungry agaim and start looking for your next snack? That's probably because you haven't eaten enough protein.<br>
Protein-rich meas take longer to digest, leaving you fuller for longer. Additionally, if you're trying to cut (lose weight), protein helps your body retain muscle mass when calories are sparse.<br>
The answer to being snatched or jacked isn't salads, it's PROTEIN.<br>


3. ***Immune Health***<br>
Your body contains cells call antibodies and T-cells. These little ouens are big fighters for your health. They identify viruses or bacteria in your body and get rid of them. <br>
What are these cells made of? You guessed it: Protein.<br>
So, having enough protein not only builds muscles, but keeps you healthy.<br>
Less sick days = More time in the gym<br>

4. ***Hormone Balance***<br>
Hormones are chemical messengers in your body that help parts of your talk to each other. A lot of important hormones are made up of proteins, and you've probably heard about them: <br>
- Insulin: This hormone tells your body to absorb glucose (sugar) into cells, instead of leaving them in your bloodstream (this is when you'll hear people say, "My sugar is high.")<br>
- Glucagon: A hormone that tells the liver to break down the glucose it stores when you need energy.<br>
-hGH (human Growth Hormone): This hormone stimulates the growth of muscles, bones and a lot of your internal organs - in simple terms, it helps make gains. <br>

I've simplified these points a lot, so if you're interested in taking a deeper look at the functions of protein, check out <a href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/functions-of-protein#TOC_TITLE_HDR_4">this article</a> by 'healthline'.<br>
